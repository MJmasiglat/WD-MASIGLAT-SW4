Different Type of Coke

This Featture Display will show you 3 types of coke that are usually consume by many people a like.

COKES

Coca-Cola Classic:
Taste: Known for its iconic, refreshing taste with a blend of caramel flavors.
Ingredients: Made with carbonated water, high fructose corn syrup (or sucrose in some regions), caramel color, phosphoric acid, natural flavors (which may include caffeine).
Calories: Contains calories from sugar.
Packaging: Typically available in cans, glass bottles, and plastic bottles.
Availability: Widely available worldwide.

Diet Coke:
Taste: Offers a lighter taste compared to Coca-Cola Classic, with a distinct flavor profile due to artificial sweeteners.
Ingredients: Contains carbonated water, caramel color, phosphoric acid, natural flavors, aspartame (or other artificial sweeteners), potassium benzoate (to protect taste), potassium citrate, and caffeine.
Calories: Low-calorie or zero-calorie, depending on regulations and formulation.
Packaging: Available in cans and bottles similar to Coca-Cola Classic.
Availability: Generally available alongside Coca-Cola Classic in most markets.

Coca-Cola Zero Sugar:
Taste: Designed to closely replicate the taste of Coca-Cola Classic but without sugar.
Ingredients: Contains carbonated water, caramel color, phosphoric acid, aspartame (or other artificial sweeteners like acesulfame potassium), potassium benzoate (to protect taste), natural flavors, potassium citrate, caffeine.
Calories: Typically zero calories per serving.
Packaging: Similar packaging options as Coca-Cola Classic and Diet Coke.
Availability: Available in many markets as an alternative for those looking to reduce sugar intake while enjoying the taste of Coca-Cola.
